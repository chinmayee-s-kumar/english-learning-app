📘 Grammar Lesson

Lesson 1: Nouns and Pronouns

1️⃣ Nouns

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea. Nouns are the building blocks of sentences.

Types of Nouns

  • Common Nouns: general names of people, places, or things. dog, city, teacher, school
  • Proper Nouns: specific names. Chinmayee, Bangalore, Amazon
  • Concrete Nouns: things you can see, touch, smell, hear, or taste. book, apple, music
  • Abstract Nouns: ideas, qualities, or feelings. love, happiness, courage
  • Collective Nouns: a group of people, animals, or things. team, flock, class
Examples:
1. The dog is barking. (Common Noun)
2. Chinmayee is learning English. (Proper Noun)
3. I felt happiness yesterday. (Abstract Noun)
4. The team won the match. (Collective Noun)
💡 Tip: Proper nouns always start with a capital letter.
❌ Common Mistake: Writing proper nouns in lowercase. Example: “chinmayee is happy” → ❌
✅ Correct: “Chinmayee is happy”

2️⃣ Pronouns

A pronoun is a word that replaces a noun in a sentence. It helps avoid repetition.

Types of Pronouns

  • Personal Pronouns: I, you, he, she, it, we, they
  • Possessive Pronouns: mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs
  • Demonstrative Pronouns: this, that, these, those
  • Reflexive Pronouns: myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, themselves
  • Interrogative Pronouns: who, whom, whose, which, what
  • Relative Pronouns: who, whom, whose, which, that
Examples:
1. Chinmayee loves her dog. → She loves him.
2. This book is mine.
3. They are going to the park.
4. Who is coming to the party? (Interrogative Pronoun)
💡 Tip: Always make sure the pronoun agrees with the noun in number and gender. Example: “The boy lost his pen.” ✅
❌ Common Mistake: Using the wrong pronoun. Example: “The girl lost his bag.” → ❌
✅ Correct: “The girl lost her bag.”

3️⃣ Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: Identify the nouns in the sentence below:

"Chinmayee and her dog went to the park to play."

Exercise 2: Replace the nouns with pronouns:

"Chinmayee likes the book. Chinmayee reads the book every day."

Exercise 3: Identify the pronouns in this sentence:

"He gave his book to her because she forgot hers."

Answers:

Exercise 1: Chinmayee, dog, park

Exercise 2: She likes it. She reads it every day.

Exercise 3: He, his, her, she, hers

💡 Tip: Doing exercises regularly will help you remember nouns and pronouns quickly.
