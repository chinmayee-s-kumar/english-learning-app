Lesson 1: Nouns and Pronouns

1ï¸âƒ£ Nouns

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea. Nouns are the building blocks of sentences.

Types of Nouns

  • Common Nouns: general names of people, places, or things. dog, city, teacher, school
  • Proper Nouns: specific names. Chinmayee, Bangalore, Amazon
  • Concrete Nouns: things you can see, touch, smell, hear, or taste. book, apple, music
  • Abstract Nouns: ideas, qualities, or feelings. love, happiness, courage
  • Collective Nouns: a group of people, animals, or things. team, flock, class
Examples:
1. The dog is barking. (Common Noun)
2. Chinmayee is learning English. (Proper Noun)
3. I felt happiness yesterday. (Abstract Noun)
4. The team won the match. (Collective Noun)

A pronoun is a word that replaces a noun in a sentence. It helps avoid repetition.

Types of Pronouns

  • Personal Pronouns: I, you, he, she, it, we, they
  • Possessive Pronouns: mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs
  • Demonstrative Pronouns: this, that, these, those
  • Reflexive Pronouns: myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, themselves
  • Interrogative Pronouns: who, whom, whose, which, what
  • Relative Pronouns: who, whom, whose, which, that
