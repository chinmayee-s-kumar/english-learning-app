Lesson 2: Verbs and Tenses

1️⃣ Verbs

A verb is a word that describes an action, occurrence, or state of being. Verbs are essential for forming sentences.

Types of Verbs

  • Action Verbs: show an action. run, eat, write, play
  • Linking Verbs: connect the subject to a subject complement. am, is, are, was, were, become
  • Helping (Auxiliary) Verbs: help the main verb to form tenses, questions, negatives. is, are, have, has, do, did
Examples of Verbs:
1. She runs every morning. (Action Verb)
2. He is a good student. (Linking Verb)
3. They have finished their homework. (Helping Verb)
💡 Tip: Every sentence must have at least one verb.
❌ Common Mistake: Leaving out verbs in sentences.
Example: “She very smart.” → ❌
✅ Correct: “She is very smart.”

2️⃣ Tenses

Tenses show the time of an action. There are three main types:

  • Present Tense: action happening now or regularly.
  • Past Tense: action that happened in the past.
  • Future Tense: action that will happen.

Present Tense

  • Simple Present: I eat, He plays, They work
  • Present Continuous: I am eating, He is playing
  • Present Perfect: I have eaten, She has played
  • Present Perfect Continuous: I have been eating, They have been working
Examples:
1. I eat rice every day. (Simple Present)
2. She is eating breakfast now. (Present Continuous)
3. They have finished their homework. (Present Perfect)

Past Tense

  • Simple Past: I ate, He played
  • Past Continuous: I was eating, They were playing
  • Past Perfect: I had eaten, She had played
  • Past Perfect Continuous: I had been eating, They had been working
Examples:
1. He played football yesterday. (Simple Past)
2. They were playing outside when it rained. (Past Continuous)
3. I had eaten before they arrived. (Past Perfect)

Future Tense

  • Simple Future: I will eat, She will play
  • Future Continuous: I will be eating, They will be playing
  • Future Perfect: I will have eaten, He will have finished
  • Future Perfect Continuous: I will have been eating, They will have been working
Examples:
1. I will eat dinner at 8 PM. (Simple Future)
2. She will be studying at 6 PM. (Future Continuous)
3. By next year, they will have completed the project. (Future Perfect)

3️⃣ Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: Identify the verbs and their tenses:

"She is reading a book while he played football yesterday."

Exercise 2: Fill in the blanks with correct tense:

"I ___ (eat) breakfast at 7 AM every day."

"They ___ (finish) their homework by 5 PM yesterday."

"We ___ (go) to the park tomorrow."

Answers:

Exercise 1: is reading (Present Continuous), played (Simple Past)

Exercise 2: eat (Simple Present), had finished (Past Perfect), will go (Simple Future)

💡 Tip: Practice tenses daily using sentences about your own life. This helps you remember them easily.
